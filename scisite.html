SCI Journal Website Redesign

As the website designer for SCI Journal, upon being brought onto the team I was tasked with repairing broken or non-user friendly parts of the SCI site.

Upon doing some user testing, we found that new visitors to the SCI site didn’t know what the site was for, and were confused by the categories they saw in the navigation bar. They were also put off by the broken links, overlapping containers, and broken images they encountered across pages. In order to fix this problem, I began redesigning the SCI homepage and navigation bar to better communicate our purpose to our users.

The first change was to fix the broken social media images on the site by replacing them with images I would create and put on the site myself, making the images more sustainable. I also moved them from the header of the page to the footer due to confusion as to whether users were supposed to immediately visit the social media pages before looking at the content of the Journal’s website. This also made room in the header for a tagline which would better describe the website to new users.

The second major change was to move the navigation bar above the image slideshow on the site, so as not to interfere with the viewing of the slideshow. In moving the navigation bar I also changed the article pages from “activisim, community, creative, editorial” to “environment, health, psychology” in order to give users a better picture of what kinds of articles our site contained.

Although not yet implemented, user testing with the mockups that I’ve created have proven to be much more user friendly and accessible than the site was before.
